Section 08

Brand Voice & Content Strategy

How Shiroi Energy speaks — deep dive with real examples for social media, proposals, and client conversations. Not just tone pillars, but actual language you can use.

The Shiroi Persona
"Not a salesperson. Not a marketer.
A knowledgeable engineer who happens to be excellent at explaining things simply."

Think: the kind of expert whose advice you actually trust — because they never oversell, they quote real numbers, and they've seen every problem before. This is the voice of every Shiroi communication, from a WhatsApp message to a 40-page proposal.

Voice Pillar 1: Expert — Specificity Builds Credibility
✗ Weak — any solar company could say this
We offer the best solar solutions at competitive prices.
Our team of experts will help you go solar today.
Solar energy is the future — join us!
✓ Strong — specific, credible, earned
A 10kW rooftop system in Chennai generates approximately 1,350–1,500 units per month. At ₹8/unit, that's ₹1.28–1.44 lakh in annual savings on a system that costs ₹5–5.5 lakh installed. Payback: 3.8–4.3 years.
We've completed 500+ installations across Tamil Nadu — from 1kW home systems to 400kW industrial plants. The sizing methodology doesn't change: we start with 12 months of your electricity bills, not a catalogue.
Our O&M contracts include quarterly cleaning, inverter health checks, and performance ratio monitoring. If your system underperforms by more than 10% against the design baseline for two consecutive months, we investigate at no charge.
Why this works: Numbers anchor trust. Specifics show expertise. Limits and conditions signal honesty — and make every claim more believable.
Voice Pillar 2: Trustworthy — Say What Others Won't
✗ Weak — sounds like every solar company
We guarantee 100% customer satisfaction!
Get zero electricity bills with solar!
Subsidy will be processed quickly — no worries!
✓ Strong — acknowledges reality, builds deeper trust
Will your bill go to zero? Probably not entirely. What you'll see is a 70–90% reduction for most residential customers in Chennai. The remaining 10–30% covers grid consumption during nights, cloudy months (November–January), and usage spikes. We'll show you the exact projection for your home before you sign anything.
Subsidy processing through TANGEDCO typically takes 45–90 days after commissioning. We handle all the paperwork — form submissions, technical inspections, meter change — but the timeline is outside our control. We'll keep you updated at every stage.
A grid-tied system without battery storage switches off during load shedding — this is a safety regulation to protect linemen on the grid. Battery storage solves this but adds cost. We'll show you both options with the numbers.
Why this works: Clients have been burned by oversellers. Being the first to bring up the catch is a competitive advantage. Acknowledging limitations builds more trust than avoiding them.
Voice Pillar 3: Purposeful — Make the Impact Tangible
✗ Weak — vague green-washing
Solar is good for the planet. Join the green revolution!
Be a part of the sustainable future.
✓ Strong — real numbers that make impact personal
Your 10kW system will prevent approximately 15–17 tonnes of CO₂ emissions per year. Over 25 years, that's the equivalent of planting 625 trees — that you never have to plant, water, or maintain.
Shiroi's installed base — 10,000+ kW across Tamil Nadu — generates roughly 15 million units of clean electricity annually. That's enough to power 12,500 Chennai homes for a year, running on sunlight that would otherwise be wasted.
When your system generates more than you consume, the grid buys it back. You're not just saving — you're contributing to the grid that your neighbours draw from.
Why this works: Real numbers localised to Chennai. The pivot from "you save money" to "your surplus powers your neighbours" transforms a financial transaction into a civic contribution.
Voice Pillar 4: Direct — Lead With the Answer
✗ Weak — corporate hedging, wastes the reader's time
We would be more than happy to arrange a site visit at your earliest convenience to assess the feasibility of a potential solar installation at your premises, subject to technical suitability.
✓ Strong — answer first, reasoning second
Book a site visit. It's free, takes 45 minutes, and at the end you'll have a detailed proposal — system size, output projection, cost, ROI, and subsidy amount. No commitment required.
Short answer: yes, your roof can probably take it. Most RCC rooftops in Chennai handle solar loads without structural reinforcement. We'll confirm with a physical inspection.
The most common reason solar systems underperform is dirt. Monthly cleaning in summer, fortnightly during kambam season. It takes 20 minutes and directly affects your savings.
Why this works: Leads with the answer. Short sentences. Action-oriented. Treats the reader as an intelligent adult who doesn't need preamble.
Content Strategy by Channel
LinkedIn — B2B, Authoritative

Like a senior engineer presenting to a boardroom. Data-led, no exclamation marks, no emoji. Start with a number or a fact — never a greeting.

Example Post (150 words max)
"A 100kW commercial rooftop in T.Nagar saves approximately ₹18–22 lakhs annually at current TANGEDCO tariff rates. That's a 3.2-year payback on a 25-year asset.

We commissioned three such systems last quarter — manufacturing unit in Ambattur, logistics warehouse in Sriperumbudur, IT campus in OMR.

The pattern is consistent: facilities consuming above 2,000 units/month are where solar ROI becomes unambiguous. Below that threshold, the numbers still work — the payback just extends to 4–5 years.

If your facility is in this range, the calculation is straightforward. DM us the last 3 months of EB bills and we'll send back a projection within 24 hours."

Conversational but precise. Like a consultant on a call — friendly, but every answer grounded in facts. No emoji overload.

For a 3BHK in Adyar with a ₹4,000/month bill, a 5kW system would be the right fit — generating around 650–700 units/month and covering about 80% of your consumption.

Cost: ₹2.8–3 lakhs installed. TANGEDCO subsidy: ₹78,000 (for residential up to 3kW) or ₹18k/kW above that. Net payback: around 4 years.

Can I arrange a free site visit this weekend? I can confirm the roof assessment and finalise the numbers."
Headline Formula

Never "Go Solar with Shiroi." Instead: [specific outcome] for [specific customer] in [specific context]

₹0 electricity bill: how a Chennai school reduced energy costs by 94%
From 35kW to zero grid dependence: Velachery warehouse case study
Why net metering in Tamil Nadu pays back faster than Karnataka
10,000 kW installed: what Shiroi's Tamil Nadu customers have collectively saved
The 45-minute site visit that changed a factory's P&L
How to Handle Common Objections
The upfront cost is too high.
The system pays for itself in 3.8–4.5 years. After that, you're earning free electricity for 20+ years. Would you decline a fixed deposit that returned 23% annually? That's what solar delivers, priced correctly. And with TANGEDCO subsidies, the net cost after incentives is significantly lower than the sticker price.
I'll wait until panel prices drop further.
Panel prices have dropped 80% since 2012 and are now largely stable — the major cost driver today is inverters and installation, not panels. The variable that keeps changing is your electricity tariff, which increases 5–8% annually. Every year you wait, the payback calculation improves for solar — because your bill gets worse.
My neighbour had problems with their solar system.
Most problems trace back to three things: wrong sizing, poor installation quality, or no ongoing O&M. We'd rather tell you the system doesn't make sense for your situation than install something that won't perform. Can I ask what problems your neighbour had? Often we can diagnose the cause.
Will it work during power cuts?
A grid-tied system without battery storage switches off during load shedding — this is a mandatory safety regulation to protect linemen working on the grid. Battery storage solves this but adds ₹80,000–1.5 lakhs to the cost. We'll show you both options with the numbers and help you decide.
What if Shiroi closes down? Who services my system?
Fair question. The panels and inverters carry manufacturer warranties (25 years and 5–10 years respectively) that are honoured directly by the manufacturer regardless of the installer. We also document every installation in detail so any competent electrician can maintain it. That said, Shiroi has been operating since 2013 — we're not going anywhere.

Section 11

Website Audit

shiroienergy.com vs this brand guide — assessed on 21 March 2025. The site is built on Wix and has significant brand alignment issues that will need to be addressed in a redesign.

⚠️
Overall Assessment: Significant Redesign Required

The current website does not reflect this brand guide on typography, colour, voice or layout. The good news: the content assets are there — 500+ projects, real testimonials, blog posts. What's missing is design quality, brand consistency and conversion-focused copy. A full rebuild on a proper platform (Next.js, Webflow, or similar) is strongly recommended.

🔤
Typography
Font families and sizing
✗ Fails
The site uses Wix default fonts — none of the three brand fonts (Rajdhani, Oswald, Inter) appear anywhere on the website. The company name in the header uses what appears to be a serif or generic sans-serif. Body text is set at inconsistent sizes.
Recommendation: Implement Rajdhani Bold for the company name everywhere, Oswald for section headers, Inter for body. These are Google Fonts and can be loaded in any platform. This is a day-one fix in any rebuild.
🎨
Colour Palette
Brand colours and consistency
✗ Fails
The site does not use #00B050 (Shiroi Green) as the primary interactive colour — links and buttons appear in a different shade. The hero section uses a generic white background with a stock photo. No gradients appear anywhere. The footer background does not match the brand-specified dark forest gradient.
Recommendation: Apply the full colour token system. Hero must use the Forest Dark gradient or a strong brand-aligned visual. All CTAs must use #00B050. The footer must use #0C1A0E with #8CC88C text.
📝
Brand Voice & Copy
Headline quality and specificity
✗ Fails
"Our bright ideas rise from the sun" — the hero headline is a generic solar industry cliché. The stat section (500+ projects, 10,000+ kW) is buried at the bottom of a very long page — these numbers should lead. The FAQ answers are technically correct but written in generic web copy style, not the Shiroi voice.
Recommendation: Rewrite the hero headline using the Shiroi formula: specific outcome, specific customer, specific context. Move the impact stats (500+ projects, 23,171 trees) to the hero or just below. Rewrite the FAQ answers using the brand voice principles in Section 08.
📐
Layout & Navigation
Page structure and user flow
⚠ Needs Work
Navigation links include pages named "blank", "blank-3", "blank-5" — these are Wix template pages that haven't been properly renamed. The page is very long with poor content hierarchy. The Wix mobile hamburger menu is functional but not brand-aligned. There's no clear conversion path — the CTA "Start Saving" goes to a contact page, but there's no offer or reason to convert.
Recommendation: Fix navigation page names immediately — this looks unprofessional to any visitor who hovers or inspects. Redesign with a clear conversion funnel: hero CTA → site visit booking → proposal. Add a floating WhatsApp CTA button (consistent with how clients actually contact solar companies in Chennai).
📱
Mobile Responsiveness
Rendering on mobile devices
⚠ Needs Work
The Wix site renders on mobile but the hero text is too small, the stat section is cramped, and the client logo carousel doesn't scale well. The contact form is accessible but below the fold on mobile — most visitors will reach Shiroi via mobile in Chennai.
Recommendation: Design mobile-first in the redesign. Hero headline must be readable at 320px. The CTA button should be sticky on mobile scroll. WhatsApp contact is more effective than email form for Chennai residential market.
✅
Content Assets
Quality of testimonials, projects, blog
✓ Acceptable
The site has three genuine client testimonials with names and profile photos. The blog has 6+ posts. The client logo section shows real clients. The project/case study section exists (though sparse). The FAQ section addresses real objections. These assets are valuable and should be carried forward into the redesign.
